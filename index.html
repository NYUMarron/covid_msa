Understanding cities' curves

Click to expand ▼

The curve for reported cases (it is the same for reported deaths), at any given date, has five important properties.

  • Duration (A): The number of days from the first reported case until the reporting date
  • Days from Peak (B): The number of days from the peak 7-day average of new cases until the reported date
  • Peak New Cases (C): The peak 7-day average of new reported cases by the reporting date
  • Current Number of New Cases (D): The 7-day average of new cases on the reporting date
  • Total Number of Cases (E): The total number of cases by the reporting date, which corresponds to the area of the curve at the reporting date
